# brand-new-startup

An opinionated agentic stack for a founder going from idea to first paying users, solo or with a small team.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-13

## Who this is for

- **Business model:** self-serve. Customers find you, sign up, and pay with a card. Covers SaaS, PLG tools, DTC products, creator/content businesses, and light marketplaces.
- **Team:** 1–3 people, at least one technical founder (or a founder willing to drive a coding agent).
- **Budget:** bootstrapped. Free tiers and usage-based pricing preferred. Paid tools only when they replace a human.
- **Target timeline:** first paying user within 30 days of starting.

## Who this is NOT for

- **B2B sales-led** businesses where humans close humans — needs a Sales substack (not yet built).
- **Regulated industries** (health, finance, legal) with compliance tooling requirements.
- **Services / agencies** where you sell your time — different economics, different tools.
- **Hardware / physical products** — needs supply chain and fulfillment substacks.

## Substacks

- [tech/](tech/) — product, infra, payments, observability. Everything needed to put a working thing on the internet and collect money.
- [marketing/](marketing/) — brand, landing page, content, SEO, email, analytics. Everything needed to get traffic and convert it.

Tech and marketing are **sufficient** for this archetype because self-serve checkout replaces a sales team and usage-based tooling replaces most operational work. If your business doesn't fit, this isn't the right stack.

## How to use this stack

1. **Read [tech/](tech/README.md) end-to-end first.** It sets up the repo and payment rails. Timeline: 1–2 weeks with a coding agent.
2. **Then [marketing/](marketing/README.md).** Brand, landing page, first content, SEO foundations. Timeline: ~1 week, ideally overlapping with tech.
3. **Launch.** Product Hunt / HN / niche community + personal network.
4. **Iterate** based on the three numbers: visitors, signups, paying users.

Both substacks assume you will use a coding/agent tool (Claude Code, Cursor) as your primary execution environment rather than writing everything by hand.
